About Me

I'm a sophomore at MIT studying CS and Physics. Currently, I'm researching agents for automated bias detection in vision-language models at the MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory.

My research interests are still evolving, but generally I want to understand how artificial intelligent systems learn and reason efficiently from data, as well as what we can learn from the representations they construct of our world. Previously, I have also conducted research in integrated photonics for nonlinear/quantum optics and neural network acceleration.

In my free time, I like to read, play volleyball, and slowly but surely taste test every matcha drink in Boston. If you'd like to chat about any of the above or just want to say hi, feel free to reach out!
